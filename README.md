# PHY407-Labs
A collection of selected projects for PHY407 at the University of Toronto

Lab topics are as follows:

Lab 1: Modeling a planetary orbit

Lab 2 Q1: Exploring numerical issues with standard deviation calculations - compounding rounding errors

Lab 2 Q3: Numerical integration of electrostatic line charge

Lab 3 Q2: Determining the period of a relativistic particle on a spring with numerical integration and deviation caused by singularities - the efficacy of different methods is investigated.

Lab 3 Q3: Investigation of numerical differentiation errors

Lab 4 Q1: Solving linear systems

Lab 5 Q1: Mutating audio files with FFTs and other applications of the Fourier transform to integration.

Lab 5 Q2: Revisiting the relitivistic spring

Lab 6 Q1: Modeling Stick-slip friction

Lab 7 Q3: Modeling Oscilating Chemical Reactions

Lab 8 Q1: Tempurature distrobution in a conductor (Application of PDE methods)

Lab 8 Q3: Solving Burgers' wave equation (Leapfrog for PDEs)

Lab 9 Q1: solving the wave equation with spectral methods

Lab 9 Q3: Solving Burgers' wave equation (with Lax-Wendroff)

Lab 10 Q2: Modeling limb darkening of a star (Monte-Carlo)

Lab 10 Q3: Importence sampling (Monte-Carlo-integration)

Lab 11 Q1: Monte-Carlo simulation of a gas
